Manokwari City
West Papua

Manokwari is a city located in West Papua Province, Indonesia. This city is the capital of the province. Located on the north coast of Papua Island, Manokwari has a number of stunning natural beauties, such as white sandy beaches, tropical rainforests and mountains. The city of Manokwari has a rich history, as the place where Dutch missionaries and explorers arrived in the 19th century. One important historical point was when the Red and White flag was first raised over the land of Papua by Soetan Boelaard in 1945. As the provincial capital, Manokwari is the center of government and economy in West Papua. The city also has several educational and health facilities, and is a base for trade and tourism activities in the region. The city has a variety of cultural events, art exhibitions and festivals that showcase the richness of Papuan culture.

Pictures of tourist attractions in Manokwari

White sand beach

Pasir Putih Beach is a beach which is a tourist attraction in East Manokwari District, Manokwari Regency. This beach is named so because it has clean and fine white sand.

Mansinam Island

Mansinam Island is an island that provides religious tourism for Protestant Christians in Papua. This island is part of the West Papua region. It is located about 12 kilometers from Manokwari City. To reach this island it only takes 10 to 15 minutes using an engine boat.

Bald Mountain

Mount Botak is a unique mountain in South Manokwari, West Papua which must be explored while on holiday. From the top you can enjoy a enchanting view of the expanse of the sea.

Amban Beach

Amban Beach is a beach at the northeastern head of the Doberai Peninsula, Papua Province, Indonesia. This beach is located 3 kilometers (1.9 mi) north of Amban village and 7 kilometers (4.3 mi) north of Manokwari.

Lemon Island

Lemon Island or Wappi is a small island located in the waters of Manokwari Regency. You can travel from the center of Manokwari City using a small boat and takes around 10 minutes.

Bakaro Beach

Bakaro Beach is located on the North coast of Manokwari, located adjacent to Pasir Putih Beach. The uniqueness of this beach lies in the tradition of calling fish. Calling fish is usually done by residents living around this beach to attract visitors.
